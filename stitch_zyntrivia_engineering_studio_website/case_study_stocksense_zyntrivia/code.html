CASE STUDY

Inventory that knows what's expiring, and where.

Reducing commercial food waste through predictive batch tracking and automated replenishment logic.

Next.js PostgreSQL Prisma

— THE PROBLEM

Enterprise food distributors were losing 14% of annual revenue to inventory expiration. The existing systems relied on manual auditing, leading to "ghost stock" where items expired at the back of shelves because the software only tracked quantity, not placement or batch age.

The challenge was to build a system that didn't just count items, but understood the 3D topology of a warehouse and the temporal decay of its contents.

— WHAT WE BUILT

StockSense is a proactive inventory management engine. It utilizes a custom "First-Expiry-First-Out" (FEFO) routing algorithm that generates pick-lists based on shelf depth and batch expiration data.

  • Predictive Alerts: Automated notifications 48 hours before batch critical windows.
  • Spatial Mapping: A digital twin of the warehouse floor visualizing heatmaps of high-turnover zones.
  • Dynamic Reordering: Integration with supplier APIs to trigger restocks only when safety stock thresholds are breached.

— ENGINEERING DECISIONS

We opted for a PostgreSQL schema utilizing recursive CTEs to handle complex warehouse hierarchies (Building > Aisle > Rack > Bin). This allowed for rapid spatial queries without the overhead of a dedicated graph database.

For the real-time dashboard, we leveraged Next.js Server Components to minimize client-side bundle size, ensuring warehouse scanners—often operating on low-bandwidth Wi-Fi—remained performant.
